Our Approach
The wellbeing of communities is often under-represented in governance, and their conditions largely underreported in the mainstream media.

Africaâ€™s rapid growth in mobile telecommunications and internet connections has given rise to instantaneous Citizen and Community Journalism, who can now share information at the touch of buttons. But this has not helped to shape their overall quality participation in inclusive government as these tools have not been deployed creatively or responsibly.

With more sophisticated political and socio-cultural organisations driving their agenda through the same platforms, citizens have become unsuspecting conduits of their agenda and conveyors of fake news. Rather than give a compelling voice, the communities have become innocent victims of activities that do not inspire government engagements. If anything, the government is consistently seeking ways to block these key digital media channels that are now available for the community to exploit in building a voice for a true participatory democracy.

Using a combination of advocacy, community relations and community journalism training techniques, MediaComms seeks to build and sustain a vital link that spotlights various communities to their government and other stakeholders in a quest for responsible and accountable interactions

.

We believe that the community are real people with an aspiration for good governance and pursuit of better living standards; only a strong voice can earn them a place on the table of inclusive governance. We exist to give reality to these aspirations. .
